Qorvo (NASDAQ: QRVO), a maker of radio-frequency semiconductor components, saw its stock price jump by over 20% on Friday. The rally was driven by stronger than expected Q2 FY’20 results (FY ends March), with adjusted EPS coming in at $1.52, compared to a consensus of $1.30 and also due to a strong forecast for the December quarter (revenue of $840 million to $860 million, compared to consensus of around $760 million). While the company is benefiting from growing 5G handset volumes, it also indicated that it saw new design wins with a major Korean smartphone maker (likely Samsung) and four Chinese phone makers. Separately, Qorvo also authorized a new $1 billion share repurchase program.
